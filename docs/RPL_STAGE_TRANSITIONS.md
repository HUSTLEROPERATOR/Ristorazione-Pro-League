# RPL Stage Transitions — Decision Framework

> Operational document. Version 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose of Stage Transitions

RPL is a meritocratic system. Stage transitions are the mechanism that makes meritocracy concrete: they ensure that every restaurant's official classification reflects its current performance, not its history.

### Why this document exists

`docs/RPL_SCORING_MODEL.md` defines what is measured and how. `docs/RPL_AUDIT_PROCESS.md` defines how evidence is collected and validated. This document defines **what happens next**: which conditions trigger a change of stage, in which direction, and under which timeline.

Without clear transition rules, RPL would be a measurement system without consequences. Stage transitions are the enforcement layer that gives the scoring model its operational meaning.

### How transitions maintain meritocracy

| Principle | How transitions enforce it |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Objective** | Transitions are triggered by numeric thresholds, not by subjective judgment |
| **Deterministic** | Given the same score and audit outcome, all restaurants receive the same decision |
| **Reversible** | A downgrade is not permanent: any restaurant can re-advance once it meets the conditions again |
| **Proportional** | Requirements increase at each stage — Excellence is harder to reach and to maintain than Lite |
| **Transparent** | Every restaurant knows the thresholds in advance; no surprise decisions |

## 2. Entry into RPL Lite

### Conditions for first admission

All of the following conditions must be met simultaneously:

| Condition | Required threshold |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Total score (self-assessment) | ≥ 35 / 100 |
| A1 — Contracts & Compliance | ≥ 14 / 20 (70%) |
| No area with a score of zero | Mandatory |
| Self-assessment checklist completed | Mandatory |
| Admission checklist submitted (`templates/Checklist_Adesione_RPL.md`) | Mandatory |

> A1 is a non-negotiable gateway: no restaurant with unregulated labor contracts may enter RPL at any level, regardless of its total score.

### Who validates entry

The entry audit for RPL Lite is a **remote documentary audit** conducted by an RPL consultant or onboarding coordinator. A physical site visit is not required at this stage. See `docs/RPL_AUDIT_PROCESS.md`, section 2.1.

### What happens after admission

- RPL Lite badge is issued
- Score and stage are published on the RPL portal
- Next scheduled review: 6 months from the admission date

---

## 3. Upgrade from Lite to Standard

### Conditions required

All of the following conditions must be met simultaneously:

| Condition | Required threshold |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Total score | ≥ 60 / 100 |
| A1 — Contracts & Compliance | ≥ 16 / 20 (80%) |
| No area below 50% of its maximum | Mandatory |
| Minimum time in RPL Lite | ≥ 12 months of active participation |
| Improvement documented on at least 3 KPIs | Mandatory |
| Light upgrade audit passed with positive outcome | Mandatory |

> A restaurant may not skip stages: it is not possible to advance directly from Pre-RPL to Standard. The 12-month minimum in Lite cannot be waived.

### Upgrade process

1. Restaurant submits a voluntary upgrade request via the RPL portal
2. RPL verifies pre-conditions (score estimate, time in Lite, KPI evidence)
3. Upgrade audit is scheduled within 30 days
4. On positive outcome: RPL Standard badge is issued and scorecard is updated

See `docs/RPL_AUDIT_PROCESS.md`, sections 2.3 and 9.4 for the full upgrade audit procedure.

---

## 4. Upgrade from Standard to Excellence

### Conditions required

All of the following conditions must be met simultaneously:

| Condition | Required threshold |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Total score | ≥ 85 / 100 |
| Score ≥ 85 for two consecutive years | Mandatory |
| A7 — Culture & Ethics | ≥ 8 / 10 (80%) |
| Zero ethical code violations | Mandatory |
| Active contribution to the RPL community | Mandatory |

### What "active community contribution" means

The restaurant must demonstrate at least one of the following:

- Mentorship of at least one RPL Lite restaurant (formal or documented)
- Participation in RPL events as a speaker or case study presenter
- Publication of at least one documented RPL case study
- Active participation in RPL working groups or standard-setting panels

### What changes at Excellence level

In addition to retaining all Standard-level benefits, Excellence restaurants take on additional obligations:

- Participation in at least 2 RPL events per year
- Hosting at least one Lite restaurant in a mentorship program
- Annual publication of a documented case study

## 5. Warning Mechanism

### Conditions that trigger a warning

A warning is issued automatically when **any one** of the following conditions is detected during a scheduled or extraordinary audit:

| Trigger | Applicable stage |
|---------|-----------------|
| Total score falls below stage threshold (< 35 for Lite, < 60 for Standard, < 85 for Excellence) | All stages |
| A1 — Contracts & Compliance < 14 / 20 | All stages |
| Any single area scores zero | All stages |
| Failure to submit a scheduled self-assessment within 30 days of deadline | Lite |
| Failure to complete a scheduled annual audit within 30 days of deadline | Standard, Excellence |

### What a warning means operationally

1. RPL sends a formal written notification to the restaurant within **5 days** of the audit
2. The RPL badge remains active but is marked **"under review"** on the portal
3. An RPL support consultant is assigned if one is not already in place
4. The **90-day recovery plan** is started automatically (see Section 6)
5. No upgrade requests may be submitted while a warning is active

---

## 6. Recovery Plan

### How recovery works

The recovery plan is a structured 90-day corrective process activated automatically upon a warning.

```
[ Warning issued ]
        ↓
[ Recovery Plan opens — Day 0 ]
  - RPL support consultant assigned
  - Critical areas identified (scoring gap analysis)
  - Corrective action plan documented and agreed
        ↓
[ Check-in at Day 45 ]
  - Progress reviewed by consultant
  - Evidence of corrective actions verified
  - Plan updated if needed
        ↓
[ Extraordinary audit at Day 90 ]
        ↓
     Score above threshold?
    ↙                      ↘
   YES                      NO
Stage maintained        Downgrade (see Section 7)
Badge confirmed         (or exit from RPL if already in Lite)
```

### What the restaurant must produce during recovery

| Item | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| Corrective action plan document | Day 7 from warning |
| Evidence of at least 2 corrective actions started | Day 30 |
| Interim self-assessment update | Day 45 (check-in) |
| Full documentary evidence package | Day 85 (pre-audit) |

### Recovery timeline constraints

- The 90-day clock starts from the date of the formal warning notification, not the date of the triggering audit
- The extraordinary audit at Day 90 must be an **on-site audit** (remote is not accepted for recovery)
- A restaurant may only run one recovery plan at a time
- A second warning within 12 months of completing a recovery plan triggers an accelerated downgrade review (no second 90-day recovery window)

---

## 7. Downgrade Conditions

### When a restaurant moves to a lower stage

A downgrade is applied when:

| Condition | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Extraordinary recovery audit (Day 90) fails — score still below threshold | Downgraded one stage |
| A1 < 14/20 confirmed at Day 90 audit | Downgraded one stage regardless of total score |
| Second consecutive failed recovery within 12 months | Downgraded one stage; second recovery window not available |
| RPL Lite restaurant fails recovery | Exits RPL (returns to Pre-RPL status) |

### How downgrade is applied

1. RPL communicates the downgrade decision in writing within **10 days** of the Day 90 audit
2. The restaurant's badge is updated on the portal immediately
3. The restaurant retains access to the previous stage's resources
4. A new recovery period is available once the restaurant meets entry conditions for the lower stage

### Downgrade is not permanent

A downgraded restaurant can re-advance to its previous stage as soon as it meets all required conditions. However:

- A restaurant may not advance more than **one stage per year**
- The 12-month minimum in Lite applies even to restaurants returning from Standard

## 8. Immediate Suspension

Certain violations trigger immediate suspension without going through the warning and recovery cycle.

### Violations that trigger immediate suspension

| Violation | Evidence required | Effective from |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| Confirmed undeclared labor (lavoro sommerso accertato) | Official administrative or judicial finding | Day of finding |
| Serious ethical code violation confirmed by RPL | Documented RPL investigation outcome | Day of confirmation |
| Fraudulent documentation submitted during an audit | Auditor report + RPL validation | Day of validation |
| Active criminal investigation involving the restaurant for labor or food safety violations | Formal judicial notification | Day of notification |

### What suspension means

- **Badge removed immediately** from the RPL portal
- All RPL benefits (visibility, rankings, market access) are suspended
- An extraordinary audit is scheduled within **7 days** (or 30 days if related to a non-criminal ethical finding)
- The restaurant may not apply for re-admission until:
  - The triggering violation is resolved or formally dismissed, **and**
  - A minimum of 6 months has elapsed since the suspension date

### Re-admission after suspension

Re-admission after a suspension is treated as a **new first admission** (see Section 2). The restaurant must meet all RPL Lite entry conditions from scratch. Previous stage history does not apply.

## 9. Decision Flow

The following table summarizes how a combination of score and audit outcome produces a stage decision.

### Decision Table

| Score | A1 Score | Any Area = 0? | Ethical Violation? | Recovery Failed? | Decision |
|:-----:|:--------:|:-------------:|:------------------:|:----------------:|---------|
| ≥ 35 | ≥ 14 | No | No | — | ✅ Confirm / Enter Lite |
| ≥ 60 | ≥ 16 | No | No | — | ✅ Confirm / Enter Standard |
| ≥ 85 | ≥ 16 | No | No (+ community contribution) | — | ✅ Confirm / Enter Excellence |
| < threshold | Any | Any | No | No | ⚠️ Warning → Recovery |
| Any | < 14 | Any | No | No | ⚠️ Warning → Recovery |
| Any | Any | Yes | No | No | ⚠️ Warning → Recovery |
| < threshold | Any | Any | No | Yes | 🔽 Downgrade |
| Any | Any | Any | Serious (confirmed) | — | 🚫 Immediate Suspension |

### Decision Flow Diagram

```
[ Audit completed ]
        ↓
[ Score calculated + conditions checked ]
        ↓
┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Serious ethical violation?          │
│  Yes → IMMEDIATE SUSPENSION          │
│  No  → continue                      │
└──────────────────────────────────────┘
        ↓
┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Score ≥ stage threshold?            │
│  A1 ≥ minimum?                       │
│  No area at zero?                    │
│                                      │
│  ALL YES → ✅ Stage confirmed         │
│  ANY NO  → ⚠️ Warning + Recovery     │
└──────────────────────────────────────┘
        ↓ (if Warning)
[ 90-day Recovery Plan ]
        ↓
[ Day 90 Extraordinary Audit ]
        ↓
┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Conditions met?                     │
│  Yes → ✅ Stage maintained            │
│  No  → 🔽 Downgrade                  │
└──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Example Transition Case: Osteria Verdi — Warning to Confirmation

**Situation:** Osteria Verdi is in RPL Standard (score 68/100). At its annual audit, the auditor finds:
- A1 score: 13/20 (below the 14/20 minimum)
- Total score: 63/100 (above the 60 threshold for Standard)

**Decision:** Despite the total score being above threshold, A1 is below the mandatory minimum. This triggers a **warning** regardless of the total score.

**Recovery plan activated — Day 0:**
- Gap identified: one staff member on an irregular temporary contract
- Corrective action: contract regularized within 14 days + HACCP documentation updated

**Day 45 check-in:**
- Contract regularized and documented; HACCP current; A1 score re-estimated at 16/20

**Day 90 extraordinary audit:**
- A1: 16/20 ✅ | Total score: 67/100 ✅ | No area at zero ✅
- **Outcome: Stage maintained. Badge confirmed as RPL Standard. Warning closed.**

## 10. Relationship With Other Documents

This document defines the **decision rules** that connect the RPL measurement system to concrete outcomes. It does not replace or duplicate the documents it references; it uses them.

| Document | Role in stage transitions |
|----------|--------------------------|
| `docs/RPL_SCORING_MODEL.md` | Provides all numeric thresholds used in this document (Sections 2–7). Defines the 7 scoring areas and the warning/recovery logic this document operationalizes. |
| `docs/RPL_AUDIT_PROCESS.md` | Defines how scores are produced and validated. The audit outcome (confirmed score + conditions checked) is the primary input to every transition decision in this document. |
| `templates/RPL_SCORECARD_TEMPLATE.md` | The scorecard is the instrument that captures the score used to make transition decisions. Its totals and area breakdowns directly trigger the conditions in Sections 2–8. |
| `docs/RESTAURANT_LIFECYCLE.md` | Defines the four stages (Pre-RPL, Lite, Standard, Excellence) whose boundaries this document governs. The lifecycle describes what each stage means; this document defines when and how a restaurant moves between them. |
