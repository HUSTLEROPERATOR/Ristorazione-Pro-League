# Pre-RPL → RPL Lite Transformation Playbook

> Operational playbook. Version 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose of This Playbook

### Why it exists

This playbook is the field guide for consultants leading an independent restaurant from Pre-RPL status to RPL Lite readiness. It translates the governance framework defined in `docs/RESTAURANT_LIFECYCLE.md` and `docs/RPL_SCORING_MODEL.md` into a concrete 90-day action sequence.

It is not a strategy essay. It is a sequence of tasks, deliverables, and checkpoints.

### When it is used

| Trigger | Use this playbook |
|---------|------------------|
| A new restaurant completes the initial diagnostic audit | Yes — start immediately after the audit debrief |
| A restaurant is in Pre-RPL and wants to apply for RPL Lite | Yes — this is the primary entry path |
| A restaurant exits RPL (downgraded from Lite) and wants to re-enter | Yes — same process applies |
| A restaurant already holds RPL Lite and is preparing for Standard | No — use the Lite → Standard playbook |

### What "Lite-ready" means operationally

A restaurant is Lite-ready when it can demonstrate:

- 100% regular and documented staff contracts
- Minimum first training hours recorded (≥ 20 hours/year target set, at least first session completed ≥ 2 hours)
- At least one active waste-reduction action (doggy bag, seasonal menu, or equivalent)
- Minimum operational documentation in place (at least opening/closing checklist and one SOP)
- Self-assessment checklist completed and signed (`templates/Checklist_Adesione_RPL.md`)
- RPL Scoring Model total score ≥ 35/100 with A1 ≥ 14/20 and no area at zero

## 2. Entry Conditions

### Typical Pre-RPL restaurant profile

| Characteristic | Typical situation |
|---------------|------------------|
| Staff contracts | Irregular, incomplete, or entirely undocumented |
| Operational documentation | None or informal notes; processes live in the owner's head |
| Training | Occasional, unstructured, never recorded |
| Food waste | No tracking, no active reduction initiative |
| Financial data | Based on receipts and intuition; no food cost monitoring |
| Customer reputation | Passive (no review management, no systematic feedback) |
| Ethics & culture | No formal code; welfare is ad hoc |

### Common problems at this stage

1. **Contract irregularity** — the single most frequent blocker; prevents RPL entry regardless of score
2. **Zero documentation reflex** — processes exist but nothing is written down; staff turnover kills institutional knowledge
3. **No training record** — training happens informally but is never tracked; hours cannot be verified
4. **Reactive waste management** — leftovers are disposed of without any active reduction strategy
5. **Data blindness** — the owner cannot state food cost, cover count trend, or average ticket with confidence
6. **Owner dependency** — the restaurant cannot function normally without the owner present for more than a shift

## 3. Minimum Transformation Targets for RPL Lite

These are the **non-negotiable targets** that must be reached before submitting the Lite application. They map directly to the scoring thresholds in `docs/RPL_SCORING_MODEL.md`.

| Area | Target | Minimum threshold |
|------|--------|:-----------------:|
| **A1 — Contracts & Compliance** | All staff on regular, documented contracts | ≥ 14 / 20 |
| **A2 — Team & Training** | Training plan established; first session completed and logged | ≥ 5 / 20 |
| **A3 — Operations & Systems** | Opening checklist + at least one core SOP (kitchen or service) | ≥ 4 / 15 |
| **A4 — KPI & Data** | Food cost tracked manually; at least one KPI recorded monthly | ≥ 3 / 15 |
| **A5 — Sustainability** | One active waste-reduction action implemented | ≥ 3 / 10 |
| **A6 — Customer Reputation** | Google Business profile claimed; first review response written | ≥ 2 / 10 |
| **A7 — Culture & Ethics** | RPL code of ethics adopted or equivalent statement signed | ≥ 2 / 10 |
| **Total** | — | **≥ 35 / 100** |

> **A1 is a hard gate.** No restaurant may apply for RPL Lite with A1 < 14/20 or with any area at zero, regardless of total score.

## 4. 30-60-90 Day Transformation Structure

### Days 1–30 — Foundation

**Goal:** Eliminate blockers. Regularize contracts. Establish first operational documentation.

| Action | Owner | Output |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Conduct diagnostic audit using `consulting-os/templates/audit-report/` | Consultant | Completed audit report + gap analysis |
| Identify all irregular or missing contracts | Consultant + Owner | Contract inventory |
| Regularize all staff contracts with qualified legal or HR support | Owner (+ external HR) | Signed compliant contracts on file |
| Claim and verify Google Business profile | Owner | Active public profile |
| Draft opening/closing checklist (use `consulting-os/templates/checklist-opening/` and `checklist-closing/`) | Consultant | Two operational checklists |
| Identify one waste-reduction action to implement immediately | Consultant + Owner | Action agreed and started (e.g., doggy bag launch) |
| Create training log template and record any training already completed | Consultant | Training log initialized |
| Present and sign RPL code of ethics or equivalent | Owner | Signed document on file |

**30-day checkpoint:** Contracts 100% regular ✅ — if not, pause and resolve before proceeding.

---

### Days 31–60 — Systems

**Goal:** Build minimum documentation pack. Start tracking first KPIs. Launch training plan.

| Action | Owner | Output |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Write first core SOP (kitchen opening or service flow) | Consultant + Chef/Manager | 1 completed SOP |
| Set up basic food cost tracking (manual spreadsheet is sufficient) | Consultant | Monthly food cost template |
| Define training plan for the next 12 months (≥ 20 hours/person target) | Consultant + Owner | 12-month training calendar |
| Deliver first staff training session (safety, hygiene, or service basics) | Owner/Manager | Training session + log entry |
| Record first monthly KPI snapshot (covers, revenue, food cost %) | Owner | Month 1 KPI data point |
| Implement the agreed waste-reduction action (doggy bag, portion review, or seasonal menu item) | Owner | First evidence of action (receipt, photo, supplier invoice) |
| Respond to 3 Google reviews as practice (positive or negative) | Owner | 3 public responses live |

**60-day checkpoint:** At least one SOP completed ✅, training log has at least one entry ✅, food cost tracked ✅.

---

### Days 61–90 — Readiness

**Goal:** Complete the self-assessment. Compile the Lite application package. Verify all thresholds are met.

| Action | Owner | Output |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Complete RPL self-assessment checklist (`templates/Checklist_Adesione_RPL.md`) | Owner (guided by consultant) | Signed self-assessment |
| Compile basic SOP pack (at minimum: kitchen opening, service checklist, one process SOP) | Consultant | SOP mini-pack (3 documents) |
| Produce compliance checklist: confirm contracts, HACCP documentation current, allergen info | Consultant | Compliance checklist |
| Record second monthly KPI snapshot | Owner | Month 2 KPI data point |
| Run internal Lite readiness review (use checklist in Section 7 of this playbook) | Consultant | Lite readiness checklist completed |
| Finalize and package all deliverables (see Section 6) | Consultant | Application package ready |
| Submit Lite application via RPL portal | Owner | Application submitted |

**90-day checkpoint:** All items in the Lite-readiness checklist in Section 7 confirmed ✅.

## 5. Required Consulting-OS Modules for This Transition

Not all consulting-os phases are required for the Pre-RPL → Lite transition. The following subset is sufficient and necessary.

| Module | Role in this transition | Priority |
|--------|------------------------|:--------:|
| **Audit** (`templates/audit-report/`) | Diagnostic: identify gaps and blockers before work begins | 🔴 Critical |
| **Checklist-Opening / Checklist-Closing** (`templates/checklist-opening/`, `templates/checklist-closing/`) | First operational documentation; enables A3 score | 🔴 Critical |
| **SOP** (`templates/sop/`) | Core process documentation; minimum one SOP needed for Lite | 🔴 Critical |
| **Team / Mansionario** (`templates/mansionario/`) | Foundation for training plan; supports A2 score | 🟡 Important |
| **KPI Basic Setup** (`kpi/`) | Manual food cost tracking; minimum A4 evidence | 🟡 Important |
| **Basic Sustainability Action** | Waste reduction initiative; activates A5 score | 🟡 Important |
| **Self-Assessment Preparation** (`templates/Checklist_Adesione_RPL.md`) | Required for Lite application submission | 🔴 Critical |

**Modules NOT needed at this stage:** marketing playbook, CRM, automations, upselling scripts, menu engineering. These are Phase 2 (Lite → Standard) work.

## 6. Deliverables Produced During the Transition

The following outputs are produced during the 90-day transformation. Each deliverable serves both as a consulting output and as evidence for the Lite application.

| Deliverable | Produced in | Primary purpose |
|-------------|:-----------:|----------------|
| **Diagnostic audit report** | Days 1–10 | Baseline assessment; gap analysis; work plan |
| **Contract compliance checklist** | Days 1–30 | Evidence of A1 compliance; blocker clearance |
| **Opening checklist** | Days 1–30 | A3 operational documentation |
| **Closing checklist** | Days 1–30 | A3 operational documentation |
| **Training log** (template + first entries) | Days 1–60 | A2 training evidence |
| **Core SOP pack** (minimum 1, target 3) | Days 31–60 | A3 operational documentation |
| **Monthly KPI sheet** (2 months minimum) | Days 31–90 | A4 KPI evidence |
| **Waste reduction evidence** (photo, receipt, or process note) | Days 31–60 | A5 sustainability evidence |
| **Self-assessment checklist** (`Checklist_Adesione_RPL.md`) | Days 61–90 | Required Lite application document |
| **Lite readiness checklist** (Section 7 of this playbook) | Day 90 | Internal confirmation before submission |
| **Lite application package** (all above bundled) | Day 90 | Submitted to RPL for entry audit |

## 7. Lite-Readiness Checklist

Use this checklist at Day 90 before submitting the RPL Lite application. All items must be confirmed.

### A1 — Contracts & Compliance

- [ ] All active staff have signed, regular, documented employment contracts on file
- [ ] HACCP documentation is current and accessible
- [ ] Allergen information is available for all menu items
- [ ] A1 estimated score ≥ 14 / 20

### A2 — Team & Training

- [ ] Training log created and has at least one recorded session
- [ ] 12-month training plan defined (≥ 20 hours/person target)
- [ ] A2 estimated score ≥ 5 / 20

### A3 — Operations & Systems

- [ ] Opening checklist written and in active use
- [ ] Closing checklist written and in active use
- [ ] At least one core SOP documented (kitchen opening, service flow, or equivalent)
- [ ] A3 estimated score ≥ 4 / 15

### A4 — KPI & Data

- [ ] Food cost tracked manually for at least 1 full month
- [ ] At least one KPI (covers, revenue, or food cost %) recorded on a monthly basis
- [ ] A4 estimated score ≥ 3 / 15

### A5 — Sustainability

- [ ] At least one active waste-reduction action in place (doggy bag, seasonal menu item, portioning review, or equivalent)
- [ ] A5 estimated score ≥ 3 / 10

### A6 — Customer Reputation

- [ ] Google Business profile claimed and verified
- [ ] At least 3 recent reviews have been responded to publicly
- [ ] A6 estimated score ≥ 2 / 10

### A7 — Culture & Ethics

- [ ] RPL code of ethics (or equivalent written statement) adopted and signed by the owner
- [ ] A7 estimated score ≥ 2 / 10

### Final Gate

- [ ] **Total estimated score ≥ 35 / 100**
- [ ] **A1 score ≥ 14 / 20**
- [ ] **No area with a score of zero**
- [ ] **Self-assessment checklist (`templates/Checklist_Adesione_RPL.md`) completed and signed**
- [ ] **All deliverables from Section 6 compiled into the application package**

> If any item above is unchecked, do not submit. Address the gap and return to this checklist.

## 8. Practical Example — Trattoria Lo Scoglio

### Starting situation (Day 0)

**Trattoria Lo Scoglio** is a family-run seafood restaurant on the Sardinian coast. The owner, Salvatore, runs the kitchen. His wife, Mirella, handles the front of house. Two seasonal staff. No formal documentation. No HR support.

**Initial diagnostic audit results:**

| Area | Score at Day 0 | Notes |
|------|:-------------:|-------|
| A1 — Contracts & Compliance | 6 / 20 | Two staff on undeclared arrangements; HACCP expired |
| A2 — Team & Training | 2 / 20 | No training records; informal onboarding only |
| A3 — Operations & Systems | 0 / 15 | Zero written documentation |
| A4 — KPI & Data | 1 / 15 | Receipts only; no tracking |
| A5 — Sustainability | 3 / 10 | Seasonal menu in use; no doggy bag |
| A6 — Customer Reputation | 5 / 10 | Google rating 4.1; no review responses |
| A7 — Culture & Ethics | 1 / 10 | No formal ethics commitment |
| **Total** | **18 / 100** | Pre-RPL — not eligible for Lite |

**Key blockers:** A1 (contracts), A3 (zero documentation).

---

### Days 1–30 — Foundation

- Salvatore works with a local labor consultant to regularize both staff contracts. Cost: ~€600 in legal fees. Completed by Day 21.
- HACCP documentation renewed with a licensed hygiene consultant. Completed by Day 28.
- Consultant produces opening and closing checklists based on observed workflows.
- Mirella adds doggy bag bags to the table setup. First waste-reduction action active.
- RPL code of ethics printed, discussed with staff, and signed by Salvatore.
- Google Business profile already claimed; Mirella responds to two reviews (guided by consultant).
- Training log template created. First informal session (allergen awareness, 90 minutes) recorded.

**30-day checkpoint:** A1 blocker cleared ✅. Contracts 100% regular.

---

### Days 31–60 — Systems

- Consultant drafts kitchen opening SOP with Salvatore over two working sessions.
- Food cost tracking sheet introduced. Salvatore records ingredient costs weekly for the first time. Month 1 food cost: 34% (above target, but now visible).
- Training plan for the year defined: hygiene refresher (Q2), service training (Q3), fire safety (Q4). Total: 22 planned hours.
- Second staff training session: service basics. 2 hours. Logged.
- Mirella responds to all new Google reviews. Rating stable at 4.2.
- Seasonal menu already in place; consultant documents it as formal sustainability evidence.

**60-day checkpoint:** First SOP completed ✅, food cost tracked ✅, training log has 2 entries ✅.

---

### Days 61–90 — Readiness

- Consultant and Salvatore complete the self-assessment together in a 2-hour session. Score estimated at 45/100 (the closing checklist and second KPI snapshot, finalized later in this phase, will bring the final total to 48/100).
- Closing checklist finalized and laminated in the kitchen.
- Second monthly KPI snapshot recorded. Cover count stable; food cost down to 31%.
- Lite readiness checklist reviewed: all items confirmed.
- Application package assembled: contracts copy, HACCP certificate, checklists, SOP, training log, KPI sheets, self-assessment, ethics statement.
- Application submitted via RPL portal on Day 88.

---

### Final score at Day 90

| Area | Score at Day 90 | Change |
|------|:--------------:|:------:|
| A1 — Contracts & Compliance | 16 / 20 | +10 |
| A2 — Team & Training | 7 / 20 | +5 |
| A3 — Operations & Systems | 6 / 15 | +6 |
| A4 — KPI & Data | 4 / 15 | +3 |
| A5 — Sustainability | 5 / 10 | +2 |
| A6 — Customer Reputation | 6 / 10 | +1 |
| A7 — Culture & Ethics | 4 / 10 | +3 |
| **Total** | **48 / 100** | **+30** |

**Outcome:** RPL Lite entry audit passed. Badge issued. Lite status confirmed.

**What made the difference:** Resolving the contract situation in Week 3 (A1 from 6 to 16) and creating even minimal written documentation (A3 from 0 to 6) drove the bulk of the score improvement.
