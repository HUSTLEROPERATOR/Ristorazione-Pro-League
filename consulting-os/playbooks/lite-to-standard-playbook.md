# RPL Lite → Standard Transformation Playbook

> Operational playbook. Version 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose of This Playbook

### Why it exists

This playbook is the field guide for consultants leading an independent restaurant from RPL Lite status to RPL Standard readiness. It translates the transition conditions defined in `docs/RPL_STAGE_TRANSITIONS.md` and the scoring thresholds in `docs/RPL_SCORING_MODEL.md` into a concrete 6–12 month transformation sequence.

It is not a compliance checklist. It is a structured improvement program.

### Lite vs Standard — the fundamental difference

| Dimension | RPL Lite | RPL Standard |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Focus** | Compliance and documentation baseline | Structured improvement and competitive positioning |
| **Score threshold** | ≥ 35 / 100 | ≥ 60 / 100 |
| **A1 threshold** | ≥ 14 / 20 | ≥ 16 / 20 |
| **Area floor** | No area at zero | No area below 50% of its maximum |
| **KPI discipline** | Basic tracking started | KPI dashboard updated monthly; improvement documented on ≥ 3 KPIs |
| **Time requirement** | None (first entry) | ≥ 12 months of active RPL Lite participation |
| **Audit type** | Remote documentary audit | Light upgrade audit (on-site or hybrid) |
| **Team** | Contracts regularized; first training sessions logged | Stable team structure; training program producing measurable results |
| **Customer experience** | Review profile claimed; basic responses | Active reputation management; loyalty actions in place |
| **Data** | Food cost tracked manually | Dashboard in regular use; data used to drive decisions |

A Lite restaurant has built a foundation. A Standard restaurant is using that foundation to improve.

### When this playbook is used

| Trigger | Use this playbook |
|---------|------------------|
| Restaurant has held RPL Lite for at least 6 months and wants to plan the Standard upgrade | Yes — start planning the transformation immediately |
| Restaurant is at or above Lite thresholds and wants to understand what Standard requires | Yes — use Section 3 as a diagnostic |
| Restaurant has just received its Lite badge and is setting 12-month goals | Yes — use this playbook to define the direction of work |
| Restaurant is in Pre-RPL or just starting the Lite transformation | No — use `consulting-os/playbooks/pre-rpl-to-lite-playbook.md` |

## 2. Typical Lite Restaurant Profile

### What a Lite restaurant looks like when it arrives at this playbook

A restaurant that has completed the Pre-RPL → Lite transition has made real progress. However, that progress is often uneven and fragile.

| Characteristic | Typical situation at Lite entry |
|---------------|--------------------------------|
| **Staff contracts** | Regularized — but HR processes still reactive; contract renewals handled case-by-case |
| **Training** | First sessions logged, a 12-month plan exists — but delivery is inconsistent; training depends on the owner's schedule |
| **Operational documentation** | Opening/closing checklists in use; 1–3 SOPs written — but staff do not always follow them; documentation is not yet a habit |
| **KPI tracking** | Food cost and covers tracked manually — but data lives in a spreadsheet that is not reviewed regularly; no trend analysis |
| **Customer reputation** | Google profile active; some review responses — but no systematic approach; no feedback loop into operations |
| **Sustainability** | One waste-reduction action active (doggy bag, seasonal item) — but no measurable impact or data |
| **Culture and ethics** | Ethics statement signed — but no welfare program, no team feedback mechanisms |
| **Revenue** | Stable but flat — the restaurant knows its numbers but does not know how to improve them |

### Common challenges at the start of this playbook

1. **Documentation exists but is not lived** — SOPs and checklists were created for the Lite application; they are not yet part of daily operations
2. **Owner still handles everything** — the restaurant cannot delegate consistently; the owner is involved in every operational decision
3. **Data without decisions** — food cost is tracked but the numbers do not drive menu changes, purchasing adjustments, or pricing decisions
4. **No marketing system** — customer acquisition is word-of-mouth and platform-dependent; there is no planned marketing activity
5. **Team instability** — turnover is still frequent; onboarding is informal despite the existence of a training log
6. **Customer experience is reactive** — reviews are responded to but there is no active program to improve the customer experience or build loyalty

## 3. Standard Targets

These are the targets a restaurant must reach — and sustain — to qualify for the Standard upgrade audit. They map directly to the thresholds in `docs/RPL_SCORING_MODEL.md`, section 5.3.

### A1 — Contracts & Compliance

| Target | Standard threshold |
|--------|:-----------------:|
| All staff on regular, documented contracts — without exception | ≥ 16 / 20 |
| HACCP documentation current and renewed on schedule | Required |
| HR processes formalized: contract renewals and onboarding handled systematically | Required |

### A2 — Team Stability

| Target | Standard threshold |
|--------|:-----------------:|
| Training program delivers ≥ 20 hours/person/year consistently, not just planned | ≥ 12 / 20 |
| Role profiles (mansionari) exist and are used in onboarding | Required |
| Staff turnover is tracked and below the sector average | Recommended |
| At least one team member can cover the owner's responsibilities for 2+ consecutive days | Required |

### A3 — Operational Consistency

| Target | Standard threshold |
|--------|:-----------------:|
| Full SOP pack covering kitchen, service, opening, closing, and one process-specific procedure | ≥ 10 / 15 |
| SOPs are in active use — not filed and forgotten; spot-check compliance is verifiable | Required |
| Internal audit or SOP review conducted at least twice per year | Recommended |

### A4 — Data Discipline

| Target | Standard threshold |
|--------|:-----------------:|
| KPI dashboard updated at least monthly with verified data | ≥ 9 / 15 |
| Food cost, labor cost, average ticket, and covers tracked consistently | Required |
| Documented improvement on at least 3 KPIs from Lite baseline | Required |
| Data used to make at least one documented operational decision per quarter | Required |

### A5 — Sustainability Actions

| Target | Standard threshold |
|--------|:-----------------:|
| At least 2 active and documented waste-reduction actions | ≥ 6 / 10 |
| Seasonal menu with ≥ 50% seasonal items OR documented local sourcing program | Recommended |
| Waste data tracked (at minimum qualitative trend) | Required |

### A6 — Customer Experience

| Target | Standard threshold |
|--------|:-----------------:|
| Google rating ≥ 4.0 maintained consistently | ≥ 7 / 10 |
| Systematic review response protocol in place (100% of new reviews responded to within 7 days) | Required |
| At least one active customer loyalty action (loyalty program, return incentive, or event) | Required |
| NPS or equivalent customer satisfaction measurement conducted at least once per year | Recommended |

### A7 — Culture and Ethics

| Target | Standard threshold |
|--------|:-----------------:|
| RPL code of ethics adopted and integrated into onboarding | ≥ 6 / 10 |
| Staff welfare program in place (not just a signed statement) | Required |
| Internal feedback mechanism for staff exists and is used | Required |

### Summary upgrade gate

| Condition | Required threshold |
|-----------|:-----------------:|
| **Total score** | **≥ 60 / 100** |
| **A1 — Contracts & Compliance** | **≥ 16 / 20** |
| **No area below 50% of its maximum** | **Mandatory** |
| **Minimum 12 months in RPL Lite** | **Mandatory** |
| **Improvement documented on ≥ 3 KPIs** | **Mandatory** |
| **Light upgrade audit passed** | **Mandatory** |

## 4. Transformation Areas — Consulting-OS Modules

This phase engages all seven consulting-os modules. Unlike the Pre-RPL → Lite transition (which used a critical subset), the Lite → Standard transformation requires the full consulting-os stack.

| Module | Role in this transition | Priority |
|--------|------------------------|:--------:|
| **Menu engineering** (`templates/menu-engineering/`) | Optimize menu contribution margins; reduce underperforming items; align menu with purchasing data | 🔴 Critical |
| **Marketing system** (`templates/marketing-playbook/`) | Create a planned, repeatable marketing calendar; move from passive to active customer acquisition | 🔴 Critical |
| **Customer retention** (`templates/crm-base/`) | Implement a basic CRM or loyalty system; build repeat customer behaviors | 🟡 Important |
| **Event formats** (`templates/event-format/`) | Use events as a revenue and reputation tool; create a calendar of recurring formats | 🟡 Important |
| **Operational SOP expansion** (`templates/sop/`) | Complete the SOP pack; verify active use through internal review; expand to all core processes | 🔴 Critical |
| **Data discipline** (`kpi/`, `templates/kpi-dashboard/`) | Upgrade from manual tracking to a structured dashboard; produce monthly reports the owner reviews and acts on | 🔴 Critical |
| **Team leadership** (`templates/mansionario/`) | Develop at least one team leader who can manage a shift independently; strengthen onboarding and training delivery | 🟡 Important |
| **Upselling systems** (`templates/upselling-scripts/`) | Equip front-of-house staff with structured upselling techniques; improve average ticket | 🟢 Optional |

**Modules carried forward from Lite (maintenance mode):** audit (`templates/audit-report/`), opening/closing checklists. These are maintained and improved but not rebuilt.

## 5. 6–12 Month Transformation Roadmap

This roadmap applies from the moment the restaurant commits to the Standard upgrade. It assumes the restaurant entered RPL Lite in good standing and is using this playbook at approximately Month 3–6 of Lite participation.

The minimum timeline to Standard upgrade from Lite entry is 12 months. This roadmap fills the transformation work within that window.

---

### Phase 1 — Operational Stabilization (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** Move documentation from paper existence to daily use. Stabilize team structure. Build data discipline.

| Action | Owner | Output |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Conduct a Lite → Standard gap analysis using the Section 3 targets | Consultant | Gap analysis report; score estimate vs Standard thresholds |
| Audit SOP compliance: are existing SOPs actually being followed? | Consultant (on-site) | SOP compliance report with specific gaps identified |
| Complete SOP pack: write all missing SOPs to cover full kitchen, service, opening, and closing workflows | Consultant + Chef/Manager | Full SOP pack (minimum 5 SOPs) |
| Formalize role profiles (mansionari) for all positions | Consultant + Owner | Updated mansionario for each role |
| Deliver 2 training sessions and update training log | Owner/Manager | 2 training log entries; training plan on track for 20 hours/year |
| Upgrade food cost tracking to include labor cost and average ticket | Owner | Updated KPI sheet with 3 variables tracked weekly |
| Ensure A1 score reaches ≥ 16/20: review any remaining contract or HACCP gaps | Owner (+ external HR/hygiene if needed) | A1 confirmed at ≥ 16/20 |

**Phase 1 checkpoint (end of Month 3):** Full SOP pack complete ✅, A1 ≥ 16/20 ✅, 3 KPIs tracked weekly ✅.

---

### Phase 2 — Revenue Optimization (Months 4–6)

**Goal:** Use data to improve margins. Launch menu engineering. Build the marketing foundation.

| Action | Owner | Output |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Conduct menu engineering analysis: classify items by popularity × contribution margin | Consultant | Menu engineering report (Stars, Plowhorses, Puzzles, Dogs classification) |
| Revise menu based on engineering analysis: reposition, reprice, or remove low-performing items | Owner + Consultant | Revised menu (printed and live) |
| Define annual marketing calendar: minimum 1 marketing action per month | Consultant | 12-month marketing calendar |
| Launch first planned marketing initiative (seasonal promotion, event, or local partnership) | Owner | First marketing action executed and documented |
| Train front-of-house staff on upselling techniques using `templates/upselling-scripts/` | Consultant + Manager | 1 upselling training session; scripts available at service |
| Record and review Month 4 KPI dashboard; compare to baseline | Owner + Consultant | Month 4 KPI report with trend analysis |
| Identify first data-driven operational decision (e.g., remove a Dog item, adjust purchasing to reduce food cost) | Owner | Decision documented in consultant notes |

**Phase 2 checkpoint (end of Month 6):** Menu engineering completed ✅, marketing calendar active ✅, average ticket or food cost showing measurable improvement ✅.

---

### Phase 3 — Customer Experience Improvement (Months 7–9)

**Goal:** Move from reactive reputation management to a structured customer experience program.

| Action | Owner | Output |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Implement systematic review response protocol (100% response within 7 days) | Owner/Manager | Response rate and response quality trackable; log of responses |
| Launch at least one customer loyalty action (return voucher, loyalty card, email capture, or equivalent) | Owner | Loyalty action live and documented |
| Set up a basic CRM structure using `templates/crm-base/`: capture repeat customer data | Consultant + Owner | CRM tool configured with first entries |
| Plan and execute at least one recurring event format (wine pairing dinner, tasting menu evening, or equivalent) | Owner + Consultant | First event executed; post-event KPI review completed |
| Conduct customer satisfaction measurement (comment cards, Google Form, or post-meal survey) | Owner | First NPS or satisfaction score collected |
| Update online presence: photos, menu text, and business description on Google Business and key platforms | Owner | Refreshed profiles on minimum 2 platforms |

**Phase 3 checkpoint (end of Month 9):** Google rating ≥ 4.0 maintained ✅, loyalty action active ✅, 1 event format executed ✅.

---

### Phase 4 — Market Positioning (Months 10–12)

**Goal:** Consolidate improvements. Produce evidence. Prepare the Standard upgrade application.

| Action | Owner | Output |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Run full internal score review against all Standard targets (Section 3) | Consultant | Updated score estimate; gap analysis for any remaining shortfall |
| Produce KPI dashboard comparing baseline (Lite entry) to current (Month 12) across all 7 KPIs | Owner + Consultant | KPI improvement report (3+ KPIs showing measurable improvement) |
| Complete the Standard readiness checklist (Section 7 of this playbook) | Consultant + Owner | Signed Standard readiness checklist |
| Finalize and package all deliverables (Section 6) | Consultant | Standard upgrade application package |
| Submit voluntary upgrade request via RPL portal | Owner | Application submitted |
| Prepare for the light upgrade audit: review evidence package, ensure all documents are accessible | Consultant | Audit preparation notes |

**Phase 4 checkpoint (Month 12):** All items in Section 7 Standard readiness checklist confirmed ✅, upgrade application submitted ✅.

## 7. Standard Readiness Checklist

Use this checklist at the end of Month 12 (or when the restaurant believes it has reached Standard targets) before submitting the upgrade request. All items must be confirmed.

### A1 — Contracts & Compliance

- [ ] All active staff have signed, regular, documented employment contracts on file — with no exceptions
- [ ] HACCP documentation is current and was renewed on schedule (not expired or overdue)
- [ ] Allergen information is available, current, and accessible for all menu items
- [ ] Contract renewal and onboarding process is formalized (not handled case-by-case)
- [ ] **A1 estimated score ≥ 16 / 20**

### A2 — Team & Training

- [ ] Training log is up to date; ≥ 20 hours/person recorded for the year
- [ ] Role profiles (mansionari) exist for all positions and are used in onboarding
- [ ] At least one team member can independently manage a full service shift without the owner
- [ ] **A2 estimated score ≥ 12 / 20**

### A3 — Operations & Systems

- [ ] Full SOP pack covers: kitchen opening, kitchen closing, service flow, opening checklist, closing checklist, and at least one process-specific SOP
- [ ] SOPs are in active daily use — verified by on-site check or staff confirmation
- [ ] At least one internal SOP review has been conducted in the past 6 months
- [ ] **A3 estimated score ≥ 10 / 15**

### A4 — KPI & Data Discipline

- [ ] KPI dashboard is updated monthly with verified data (not estimates)
- [ ] Food cost, labor cost, average ticket, and covers tracked consistently for ≥ 6 months
- [ ] At least 3 KPIs show documented improvement vs Lite baseline
- [ ] At least one documented data-driven operational decision per quarter exists in the record
- [ ] **A4 estimated score ≥ 9 / 15**

### A5 — Sustainability

- [ ] At least 2 active, documented waste-reduction actions are in place
- [ ] Seasonal menu or documented local sourcing program is active
- [ ] Waste trend is tracked (qualitative or quantitative)
- [ ] **A5 estimated score ≥ 6 / 10**

### A6 — Customer Experience

- [ ] Google rating ≥ 4.0 maintained for the past 3 months
- [ ] 100% of new Google reviews responded to within 7 days (or demonstrated consistent response protocol)
- [ ] At least one active customer loyalty action is in place and documented
- [ ] At least one event format has been executed and reviewed
- [ ] Customer satisfaction measurement conducted at least once
- [ ] **A6 estimated score ≥ 7 / 10**

### A7 — Culture & Ethics

- [ ] RPL code of ethics is integrated into onboarding — not just signed, but communicated to every new team member
- [ ] Staff welfare program in place (flexible scheduling, clear communication of rights, or equivalent)
- [ ] Internal feedback mechanism for staff exists and has been used at least once
- [ ] **A7 estimated score ≥ 6 / 10**

### Final Gate

- [ ] **Total estimated score ≥ 60 / 100**
- [ ] **A1 score ≥ 16 / 20**
- [ ] **No area below 50% of its maximum score**
- [ ] **Minimum 12 months of active RPL Lite participation confirmed**
- [ ] **KPI improvement report shows ≥ 3 KPIs improved vs Lite baseline**
- [ ] **All deliverables from Section 6 compiled into the upgrade application package**

> If any item above is unchecked, do not submit. Identify the gap, address it with the consultant, and return to this checklist.

## 8. Example Transformation — Osteria Centrale

### Starting situation (Lite baseline — pre-transformation)

**Osteria Centrale** is a 40-cover trattoria in a medium-sized Italian city. The owner, Gabriella, manages the dining room. Her son Marco runs the kitchen with two full-time cooks and three part-time servers. The restaurant entered RPL Lite 4 months ago after completing the Pre-RPL → Lite playbook.

**Score at Lite entry (used as baseline for this transformation):**

| Area | Lite baseline score | Notes |
|------|:------------------:|-------|
| A1 — Contracts & Compliance | 15 / 20 | All contracts regular; HACCP due for renewal in 6 months |
| A2 — Team & Training | 8 / 20 | Training log has 3 sessions; no role profiles written |
| A3 — Operations & Systems | 6 / 15 | 3 SOPs and 2 checklists; not consistently followed |
| A4 — KPI & Data | 5 / 15 | Food cost tracked; covers and average ticket not tracked |
| A5 — Sustainability | 4 / 10 | Doggy bag active; no seasonal menu data |
| A6 — Customer Reputation | 6 / 10 | Google 4.1; reviews responded to occasionally |
| A7 — Culture & Ethics | 3 / 10 | Ethics statement signed; no welfare program |
| **Total** | **47 / 100** | **RPL Lite — 13 points below the Standard threshold of 60** |

**Key gaps vs Standard (60 required, 47 current = 13-point gap):** A2 (needs +4 minimum to reach 12/20), A3 (needs +4 minimum to reach 10/15), A4 (needs +4 minimum to reach 9/15), A6 (needs +1 minimum to reach 7/10), A7 (needs +3 minimum to reach 6/10).

---

### Phase 1 — Operational Stabilization (Months 1–3)

- Consultant conducts a gap analysis using the Section 3 targets. Identifies A4 and A2 as the highest-priority gaps.
- On-site SOP compliance check reveals that the kitchen opening SOP is followed; the service SOP is not used by servers. Gap documented.
- Consultant and Marco rewrite the service flow SOP over two sessions, simplifying it for practical use. Two new SOPs added: kitchen closing and an allergen management protocol.
- Gabriella creates role profiles for all five staff members. Server onboarding now follows a structured 2-day induction.
- HACCP documentation renewed (Month 2). A1 rises to 17/20.
- KPI tracking expanded: labor cost and average ticket added to the weekly sheet. By Month 3, all four core KPIs tracked consistently.
- Two training sessions delivered: allergen awareness and service standards. Training log updated.

**Phase 1 checkpoint:** Full SOP pack (5 SOPs + 2 checklists) complete ✅, A1 = 17/20 ✅, 4 KPIs tracked weekly ✅.

---

### Phase 2 — Revenue Optimization (Months 4–6)

- Consultant runs a full menu engineering analysis using 3 months of sales data. Identifies 4 "Dog" items (low popularity, low margin — candidates for removal) and 3 "Puzzle" items (low popularity, high margin — candidates for repositioning through description or placement changes). See `templates/menu-engineering/` for the full matrix classification framework.
- Gabriella removes 3 Dog items, reprices 2 Puzzles, and adds a seasonal pasta dish recommended by the analysis.
- Marketing calendar defined: wine pairing dinner (Month 5), local school field trip partnership (Month 6), social media content plan (monthly).
- Front-of-house upselling training delivered. Average ticket increases from €28 to €31 over 6 weeks.
- Month 6 KPI review: food cost down from 34% to 30% (3% improvement = documented KPI gain #1). Average ticket up 10.7% (KPI gain #2).

**Phase 2 checkpoint:** Menu engineering completed ✅, marketing calendar active ✅, 2 KPIs showing measurable improvement ✅.

---

### Phase 3 — Customer Experience Improvement (Months 7–9)

- Gabriella implements a 7-day review response rule. By Month 8, 100% of new Google reviews have been responded to.
- Loyalty action launched: "Tavola Fedele" return card — fifth visit earns a complimentary dessert. 40 cards issued in the first 6 weeks.
- Basic CRM table set up in Google Sheets: name, email, last visit date, and occasion notes for regular customers and loyalty card holders.
- First recurring event: "Mercoledì del Vino" (wine pairing dinner, last Wednesday of the month). Average event revenue: €1,100 per evening.
- Post-dinner satisfaction survey introduced (paper card + QR code option). Average satisfaction score: 4.4 / 5 in first two months.
- Google rating rises from 4.1 to 4.3 by Month 9. Staff welfare initiative launched: Marco introduces a daily pre-service team briefing with an open feedback slot.

**Phase 3 checkpoint:** Google ≥ 4.0 maintained ✅, loyalty action active ✅, 1 event format executed ✅.

---

### Phase 4 — Market Positioning (Months 10–12)

- Consultant runs a full internal score review. Estimated total: 63/100 — above the Standard threshold.
- KPI improvement report produced: food cost (34% → 30% = ✅), average ticket (€28 → €31 = ✅), Google rating (4.1 → 4.3 = ✅). Three documented KPI improvements confirmed.
- Standard readiness checklist reviewed: all items confirmed ✅.
- Upgrade application package assembled and submitted at Month 12.
- Light upgrade audit scheduled and passed. RPL Standard badge issued.

---

### Final score at Month 12

| Area | Lite baseline | Month 12 | Change |
|------|:------------:|:--------:|:------:|
| A1 — Contracts & Compliance | 15 / 20 | 17 / 20 | +2 |
| A2 — Team & Training | 8 / 20 | 14 / 20 | +6 |
| A3 — Operations & Systems | 6 / 15 | 12 / 15 | +6 |
| A4 — KPI & Data | 5 / 15 | 11 / 15 | +6 |
| A5 — Sustainability | 4 / 10 | 7 / 10 | +3 |
| A6 — Customer Reputation | 6 / 10 | 8 / 10 | +2 |
| A7 — Culture & Ethics | 3 / 10 | 7 / 10 | +4 |
| **Total** | **47 / 100** | **76 / 100** | **+29** |

**Outcome:** RPL Standard badge issued. All conditions met. No area below 50% of maximum. 3 documented KPI improvements.

**What made the difference:** Expanding and activating the SOP pack (A3 +6), building consistent KPI discipline (A4 +6), and investing in team structure with role profiles and a team leader in Marco (A2 +6) drove the majority of the improvement. Menu engineering produced immediate, measurable revenue gains — food cost down 4 percentage points and average ticket up €3 within Phase 2 — which improved the financial margins available to fund ongoing training and events in Phases 3 and 4.
